Maine Coon

The largest of all cat breeds, the Maine Coon has a strong body with well-developed muscles and strong paws. They have a square face with large, wide-set ears.

General information

The fur is long and dense, the undercoat is covered with a shiny thick and waterproof top coat. The coat on the head, neck and shoulders is shorter and lengthens towards the tail and flanks. On the stomach and "breeches", the wool is thick and shaggy. There is a collar on the neck from the very base of the ears, and it is larger and heavier in males. The fur on the tail is long and falls like waves.

A tassel on the ears. The paws have tufts, so they resemble snowshoes. The Maine Coon cat breed can have more than 30 color options. The eyes are green, golden or copper - and white cats have blue or multi-colored eyes.

Nature

Maine coons have a very pleasant character, they are playful and friendly. They like to be in the company of people and make excellent pets. Perhaps because of their undemanding origin, they often curl up in the most unexpected places. They like the outdoors and need the ability to climb and simulate hunting. Maine Coons are known for their charming, quiet grunt.

Origin

The ancestors of Maine Coon cats were long-haired cats that were brought to the American state of Maine by sailors in the 1850s. They mated with local short-haired cats. Their descendants were large, had a strong body, medium-length fur and brush tails similar to a raccoon's tail, hence the name: Maine Coon ("raccoon from the state of Maine").

To protect against the harsh local winter, these cats have grown thick and dense fur. Already in the 1860s, special exhibitions of Maine Coon cats were held, and they became very popular as pets. Maine Coons were brought to Great Britain in the 1980s.
